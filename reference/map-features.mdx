---
title: "Interactive map features: tour, clusters, and modal"
description: "Reference for Sketchbook Map's interactive features: the collection toggle, city-based marker clustering, sketch modal, and guided tour through 8 cities."
---

The Sketchbook Map lets you explore two collections — hand-drawn sketchbook pages and type-specimen photographs — pinned to the locations where they were made. Markers cluster by city when you're zoomed out, expand when you zoom in, and open a full-size image modal when clicked. A guided tour steps through curated cities automatically. All interactions work with both mouse and keyboard.

<Note>
**Keyboard shortcuts:** When the sketch modal is open, use the **← left arrow** and **→ right arrow** keys to navigate between sketches. Press **Escape** to close the modal.
</Note>

## Collection toggle

The **Sketchbooks / Type Specimens** toggle sits at the bottom center of the screen. It switches the map between two independent collections:

- **Sketchbooks** — sketch drawings pinned to their drawing locations, loaded from `data.json`
- **Type Specimens** — type-photo images pinned to GPS coordinates from `type-photos/locations.json`

Switching modes immediately clears all existing markers and renders the other collection. Tour mode is only available in **Sketchbooks** mode; the **Take me on a tour** button is hidden when **Type Specimens** is active.

## Marker clustering

Sketches that fall within **20 km** of each other are grouped into a city cluster (`CITY_RADIUS_KM = 20`). This keeps the map readable when many sketches share the same city.

**Zoomed out (below zoom level 11):** Clicking a cluster marker fits the map to show all sketches belonging to that city (`EXPAND_ZOOM = 11`). The map animates to the bounding box of the city's sketches.

**Zoomed in (zoom level 11 and above):** Individual sketch markers are shown. Clicking any marker opens the sketch modal.

**Overlapping markers:** When two or more sketches share nearly the same coordinates (within approximately 100 m, `PROXIMITY_THRESHOLD = 0.001` degrees), they are spread in a small circle around their shared point. The spread radius scales with zoom level so markers remain readable and clickable at any zoom.

## Sketch modal

Clicking an individual marker at zoom 11+ opens the sketch modal. The modal displays:

- The full-size sketch or type-specimen image
- The sketch's **title** (shown below the image in small text)
- The **GPS coordinates**, formatted as decimal degrees with cardinal directions (e.g., `38.6560°N, 90.3122°W`)

**Navigation within the modal:**

- Use the **← left arrow** and **→ right arrow** keyboard keys to step through sketches in order
- Press **Escape** or click outside the modal image to close it

<Note>
  Modal navigation is keyboard-driven. Use arrow keys to move between sketches and Escape to close. There are no visible Previous / Next buttons in the default UI.
</Note>

## Tour mode

Tour mode is available in **Sketchbooks** mode only. The **Take me on a tour** button slides in from the top of the screen 10 seconds after the page loads. Clicking it starts a guided tour through eight curated cities.

**Tour cities, in order:**

1. Copenhagen
2. Oslo
3. Arles
4. Florence
5. London
6. Guadalupe Mountains National Park
7. San Francisco
8. St. Louis

For each city the map flies to a bounding box that fits all sketches within that city's radius. A tour bar appears at the top of the screen showing the current city name.

**Tour navigation:**

- Click **←** to go to the previous city
- Click **→** to go to the next city
- Click **×** in the tour bar to exit the tour and return to the full map view
- Switching to **Type Specimens** mode also exits the tour

<Tip>
You can click any individual marker while the tour is active to open the sketch modal without interrupting the tour.
</Tip>
